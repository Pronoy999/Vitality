AxonIQ LLP — System Overview
One holding entity, four operating units, one tech platform connecting them all
AxonIQ LLP
Holding Entity
The legal and financial parent. Owns all IP, holds the drug license, and is the contracting entity for supplier and client agreements.
Vitality Health
Unit of AxonIQ LLP · Medicines & Surgical
Sells and delivers medicines and surgical consumables directly to patients at competitive prices. Primary Phase 1 revenue engine.
AxonIQ Equipment
Unit of AxonIQ LLP · Medical Equipment
Rental, subletting, and sale of larger medical equipment — oxygen concentrators, hospital beds, mobility aids. B2B and B2C.
AxonIQ Therapies
Unit of AxonIQ LLP · Therapy Clinics
Operates clinics for physiotherapy, occupational therapy, and speech therapy. Generates recurring appointment-based revenue.
Enzo by AxonIQ
Tech Product · All Units
The unified software platform. enzoRx (digital prescription), enzoOps (order management), enzoHealth (patient data), enzoInsights (analytics). Connects all units and supply chain actors.
The core logic: Each unit independently generates revenue and captures health data as a byproduct of its operations. Enzo is the thread that connects patient data across all three units — a patient who buys medicines through Vitality Health, rents an oxygen concentrator, and attends physiotherapy sessions has their entire care journey in one place. That consolidated dataset is the long-term strategic asset.

What the System Must Do — Phase 1

  • Identify and track a sales opportunity (Opportunity ID)
  • Record who originated the lead (Originator)
  • Capture patient and client information
  • Match patient needs to products/services
  • Manage the order, procurement, and fulfilment
  • Generate bills and financial reports
  • Store structured health data with consent

What It Becomes — Phase 3+

  • AI-driven product recommendation per patient profile
  • Procurement intelligence (predict what to buy and when)
  • Drug contradiction and adherence monitoring
  • Doctor-facing clinical decision support (enzoRx)
  • Anonymised population health analytics for pharma
  • Fine-tuned LLMs on Indian health data

Plain English Guide
For the medical and operations team — how the system works without the tech jargon
What are we building and why?
AxonIQ is building a system that manages the full journey of a patient — from the moment someone refers them to us, all the way to their medicines being delivered, their therapy sessions being booked, or their equipment being rented. Along the way, the system quietly builds a health record for each patient. Over time, that record becomes valuable enough that pharma companies and hospitals will pay us for the insights it provides.
The business logic in one sentence: We make money today by selling medicines and services. We build long-term value by capturing clean health data every time we do it.
The Four Units — What Each Does

Vitality Health Phase 1

  • Sells medicines and surgical supplies directly to patients
  • Finds the best price from suppliers, passes savings to the patient
  • Manages delivery from supplier to patient's doorstep
  • Reminds patients to refill before they run out

Equipment Unit Phase 2

  • Rents or sells oxygen concentrators, beds, mobility aids
  • Serves both individual patients and small clinics
  • Tracks equipment location, condition, and return dates

Therapies Unit Phase 2

  • Books and manages physiotherapy, OT, speech therapy sessions
  • Links therapy outcomes back to the patient's health record
  • Generates recurring appointment revenue

Enzo (the tech platform) All phases

  • The software that connects all three units
  • Reads prescriptions, generates orders, tracks delivery
  • Stores patient health data securely with their consent
  • Will eventually suggest the right products for each patient automatically
How a Typical Patient Journey Works — Step by Step
1
A lead comes in
A doctor, a healthcare worker, or even the patient themselves contacts us. We create an "Opportunity" — a unique ID for this potential sale. We record who brought the lead (the Originator) because we may pay them a referral fee later.
Who does this: Medical co-founders' network, direct WhatsApp
2
We capture the patient and client details
Name, age, gender, occupation, phone. Note: the person paying (client) may be different from the patient — e.g. a family member buying for an elderly parent. We record both. We also get their consent to store and use their health data.
Who does this: Operations team via the Enzo form
3
The prescription or need is entered
Patient sends us a photo of their prescription on WhatsApp or through the app. Enzo reads the prescription automatically and creates a medicine list. If it's not sure about something, it flags it for a human to check.
Who does this: Enzo (automated) + ops team for unclear cases
4
We check for better options
If the prescription has a branded medicine, Enzo checks whether a cheaper generic with the same ingredients exists. If it does, we offer it to the patient. We also check that the new medicines won't clash with anything the patient is already taking.
Who does this: Enzo (automated check) + patient approves
5
We create the order and find the best supplier
We generate a draft order with prices. We check our registered stockists to find who has the best price. All pending patient orders are batched every 48 hours into one purchase order to the supplier — this keeps our buying volume high so we get better rates.
Who does this: Enzo creates draft, ops team confirms supplier
6
Patient receives their invoice and pays
Enzo generates a bill for the patient with the discounted price shown clearly. A payment link is sent on WhatsApp. Patient pays via UPI. Simple.
Who does this: Enzo (automated) via WhatsApp
7
Order is procured, packed, and delivered
Supplier delivers stock to us. We verify it, pack it patient-by-patient, and hand it to the delivery partner. The patient gets a tracking update at each stage: Ordered → Received → Packed → Out for delivery → Delivered.
Who does this: Operations team
8
Health data is stored. Refill is scheduled.
Once delivered, Enzo saves the prescription to the patient's health record. It also calculates when the medicines will run out and automatically sends a refill reminder before that date. The patient barely needs to think about it.
Who does this: Enzo (fully automated)
What the Medical Team's Role Is
The medical co-founders are the most critical asset in Phase 1 — not for clinical work inside the system, but for trust and distribution. Your existing patient and doctor relationships are the zero-cost customer acquisition channel that no amount of money can replace. Every doctor you know is a potential Originator. Every chronic patient in your network is a potential first customer.

What Medical Co-founders Own

  • Recruiting first 20–50 patients from existing relationships
  • Verifying prescriptions that the OCR flags as unclear
  • Reviewing drug contradiction alerts before they go to the doctor
  • Building and maintaining the disease-to-product mapping dataset
  • Training the ops team on handling prescription queries safely

What Medical Team Does NOT Need to Do

  • Write code or manage the database
  • Handle procurement or supplier negotiations
  • Manage delivery logistics
  • Build the app — that is the developer team's job
